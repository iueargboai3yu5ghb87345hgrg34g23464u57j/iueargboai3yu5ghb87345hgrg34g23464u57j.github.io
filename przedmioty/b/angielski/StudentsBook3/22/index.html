Zadanie 3

1. First, he takes a cold shower. Then, he puts on his bandana. When he enters the court, he drinks water from one bottle and then from the other. After that, he puts the two bottles next to his chair. Finally, he searches for his family members on the audience.

2. A court attendant carrying his bag to his chair.

3. Before the match begins and during every break.

4. Very important. Their presence in the crowd gives Nadal the peace of mind and strength to concentrate on the match.

5. Because he doesn't let himself be disturbed.


Fragmenty tekstu zawierające poprawne odpowiedzi:

1. 'I took a cold shower. Freezing cold water. I do this before every match. (...) Then I put on my bandana (...) I sat down, took off my white tracksuit top, and took a sip from a bottle of water. Then from a second bottle. (...) The last part of the ritual, as important as all the preparations that went before, was to look up and search for my family members among the blur of the Centre Court crowd.'

2. 'Now I was supposed to hand over my bag to a court attendant for him to carry it to my chair. (...) I don't like it. It's a break from my routine.'

3. 'I repeat the sequence, every time, before a match begins, and at every break between games, until the match is over.'

4. '...knowing they are there, as they always have been, gives me the peace of mind on which my success as a player rests. I build a wall around myself when I play, but my family is the cement that holds the wall together.'

5. 'I don't let them intrude on my thoughts during a match - I don't ever let myself smile during a match'
